Design Principles Document

Melinda H. Slaughter

Contrast

The Louvre

https://www.louvre.fr/en

The Louvre site is a great example of contrast. The dark heading and photo necessitate a light font color so that the view to be able to enhance visibility. The font color chosen was white enabling the viewer to read the font easily. This site also blends the font sized by hierarchy image placement and content are all well done.

Repetition

Laura Ingalls Wilder Museum

https://lauraingallswilderhome.com/

The Laura Ingalls Wilder Museum site uses a few elements repetitively giving the feeling of movement and consistency. The reuse of these elements is also visually appealing and unify the whole design. The color green used as well as the logo are examples of repetition in this page.

White Space and Clean Design

Family Search

https://www.familysearch.org/en/

The Family Search site is an awesome example of white space and clean design. There are very few elements on this page, but the elements that are shown do a great job of showing what the page is all about. The picture of the boy holding a picture of an ancestor are punctuated by the phrases “Find Your Family” and “Discover Yourself”. Two short sentences give the viewer a great feel for what the site is all about.
